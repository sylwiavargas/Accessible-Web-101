Designing for Inclusion

Contrary to a common belief, accessibility does not refer only to permanent conditions, impairments or disability. In fact, many of the solutions designed as assistive technology were later adapted by the general public. Did you know that electric toothbrushes were initially designed for people with weak arm muscles?

How amazing would it be if we designed everyday objects, web apps, buildings or entertainment for all users!
